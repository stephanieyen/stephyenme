I care about equitable and entrepreneurial uses of technology, especially across cities, media, and sustainability. To me, code is nothing without the humans and physical world it touches.

I naturally gravitate towards cross-functional (and chaotic) settings where I get to be at the intersection of coding, product, and customer deployment.

03 · writing

sculpting obsessions into words: essays, stories & poems

creative nonfiction research essays poetry short stories

My writing process feels like sculpting — I start with an amorphous mass and make something meaningful out of it.

I journal and write regularly about my own life — the recurring themes tend to be creativity, relationships, and seasons. I also like to write public-facing essays that break down the complexities of cities, music, and culture.

I grew up daydreaming and writing fiction (poems and short stories). This is a passion I am rediscovering. .

04 · cities

Given that I grew up in the suburbs, it might be a bit surprising that I love dense cities. I've always been intrigued by how cities reveal so much about people, systems, and culture. about

23 y/o, NYC-based human

I’m a Midwesterner at heart — I grew up in the suburbs of Cleveland, Ohio. I studied Computer Science and Urban Studies at Princeton University. During my undergraduate studies, I spent a semester abroad at the University of Edinburgh. Since then, I have traveled around Europe, Africa, and Asia, with much more to come! I am currently based in New York City.

I love to try new things. You can find me spending time in nature ("frolicking”) and cloud watching. I find joy in music, comedy, film photography / people-watching, chai lattes, wandering conversations, collaborative projects, and more — all the things.
